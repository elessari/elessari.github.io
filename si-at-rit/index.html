Supplemental Instruction @ RIT


Overview

Supplemental Instruction at RIT is an academic program that is designed to aid underclassmen in notoriously difficult courses by appointing peer leaders to engage and help currently enrolled students. The SI at RIT app was a project undertaken to help employees of the program and students alike navigate the complex directory that the program had to offered, and centralize a platform for the community to utilize.


Process

As a prior employee of the SI program, I was privy to the complex information architecture of the program and the pain points of navigating such a system. After evaluating the previous mobile application, I had decided to undergo redesigning the application. I set the goal of making the application a more centralized platform for SI, and making it easy to use for everyone, whether they were a student or an employee. Requirements gathering was accomplished by chatting with my coworkers and the students I mentored to get a gauge for possible pain points I looked over, and how a redesigned application might address these problems.

Sketching out solutions and affordances for said requirements, I made an early design choice that split my work amongst my two perceived target audiences: students and employees. The app from sketch to hi-fi was designed with two separate interfaces in mind, combined in one application under the guise of distinguishing users. Seeing as these two focus groups had differing requirements, I felt it best to accommodate their needs as directly as possible.
