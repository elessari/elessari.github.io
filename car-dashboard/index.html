Car Dashboard Project


Overview

In November of last year, I participated in a design challenge with the User Experience Club @ RIT. The challenge involved reimagining modern car dashboard and center console interfaces, utlizing data collected from a survey and analyzed for pain points and desired features. The goal of this project was to adapt popular user needs to a UI that would prioritize driver safety while providing the user with the information they wanted to see, and easier options to manipulate controls for the vehicle.


Process

Before diving into the sketching process, I decided to take a further look at the data to decide what core features of the typical car dashboard and center console most users cared about.


Based on key features, I sketched out initial ideas for the interface with modern design standards in mind, seeing as most new cars on the market now have touchscreens for the center console.


Wireframes

Working off of prior sketches, I developed wireframes for the center console and dashboard interfaces, with core features kept to the most relevant information to keep a focus on less distracted driving across the board.


Solutions

Following the general direction of my wireframes, I refined them into mid-fidelity interfaces with some components altered for usability.


For the center console, I moved away from the steering wheel controls and more into a touch-centric control system. Despite the potential for distracted driving, having a touchscreen with button controls in a car seemed to add a layer of confusion that was unnecessary to interact with the interface.


Meanwhile, for the dashboard interface I chose to adapt most features from the wireframes, and also took a new direction with the information panel to be less distracting and more informative to the user when looking at a glance.These final interfaces incorporate the overall design goals I had in mind for a reimagined car UI, while still keeping true to more modern ideas of how a car interface should work.
